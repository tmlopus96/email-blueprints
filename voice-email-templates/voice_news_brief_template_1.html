Happy Friday

Here's your weekly briefing.
Photo courtesy Jordan Griffith

College ranks high on three Princeton Review lists

We're 16th for Students who Study the Most and Best Athletic Facilities, and 19th for Most Politically Active Students. Some students are pleasantly surprised, others are skeptically surprised and still others are inspired to live out these distinctions with renewed passion. Read their reactions in full.

Ancient art acquisition
Photo courtesy Kitty Zurko

CWAM added $25,000 worth of ancient and medieval art to its collection.

The purchase was made possible by a grant from the Hewlett-Mellon Foundation. Among the acquired pieces are ancient Roman broaches, Egyptian glasswork, and Islamic leaf manuscripts. See photos and read about the rich historical and cultural significance of these artifacts.

Yung thespians
Photo by DJ Chickenhead

Winners of the Edgewood playwrighting compeition will stage their work at the College this weekend

The contest was organized by Wooster senior Eli Milette, and each play has been directed by a College theatre and dance student. Read about complete process here. The curtain goes up Saturday night at 7:00 p.m.

- Harassment by boobs

Friday morning, a student filed a sexual harrassment complaint because they were uncomfortable with the person in line behind them at MacCleod's (C-Store) having boobs.

Campus Life is investigating. No word yet on whether it was the same student who feels harassed by acknowledgement that the clitoris exists.

- Canonizing Linda Morgan Clement

The outgoing campus chaplain is now officially recognized as a saint by The College of Wooster.

President Sarah Bolton presided over the canonization ceremony, held on the dirt lot where Overholt House used to be.

- Scott Brown's hat lost

Vice president for...yada yada yada Student Affairs Scott Brown lost his iconic baseball cap early Tuesday afternoon. Brown was reportedly so distraught that he had to leave work early and did not come the next day.

Anyone with information on the whereabouts of the hat is asked to contact SPS immmediately.
